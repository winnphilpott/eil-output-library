\begin{bottomline}
{\fontsize{11pt}{15pt}\selectfont
\textbf{Offering polluters free help to follow environmental rules does not make them more likely to follow them.} Researchers studied thousands of facilities that received EPA compliance workshops and found no reduction in violations --- the program looked effective at first glance, but the improvement was a pre-existing trend, not caused by the workshops.
}
\end{bottomline}

\vspace{8pt}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
% BACKGROUND
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\sectionhead{Background}
\noindent When a polluting facility breaks environmental rules, the EPA has two main options: levy a fine, or offer a helping hand. For the past decade, the agency has been quietly shifting from the first to the second --- cutting back on inspections and penalties in favor of free \textbf{compliance assistance}: workshops and consulting designed to help facilities understand and follow the rules. The logic is appealing. But this study asks a question that had never been rigorously tested: does it actually work?

\vspace{8pt}

The black line shows what actually happened; the dashed line shows the trend that was already in motion beforehand. The two never separate. If the workshops worked, the black line would have dropped below the dashed one.}\par}

\vspace{10pt}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
% TAKEAWAY
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\sectionhead{Takeaway}
\noindent Before trading a proven enforcement tool for a newer alternative, \impllink{test whether the alternative actually works}. Inspections and penalties have a strong track record of reducing violations; compliance assistance does not. With EPA enforcement resources under pressure, scaling an unproven program comes at a real cost. The EPA has the data to run this kind of evaluation --- this study is a model for how it should be done, and a reminder that good intentions are not a substitute for evidence.

\vspace{2pt}
